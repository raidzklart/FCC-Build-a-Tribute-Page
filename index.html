Here's a timeline of Linus Torvalds' life:

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1988 - Attended the University of Helsinki.
  • 1989 - Mandatory service in the Finnish army.
  • 1990 - Returned to university and was exposed to UNIX for the first time.
  • 1991 - First prototypes of Linux were publicly released
  • 1994 - Released version 1.0 of Linux
  • 1996 - Graduated with a master's degree in computer science. His thesis was titled, Linux: A Portable Operating System.
  • 1999 - Named by the MIT Technology Review TR100 as one of the world's top 100 innovators under age 35.
  • 2004 - Moved to Dunthorpe, Oregan to be closer to the Open Source Development Labs.
  • 2010 - Became a United States citizen and registered to vote in the United States
